### RISK_TABLE

| Risk | Severity (High/Med/Low) | Technical Barrier | Market Barrier | Mitigation Path |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Extreme Wafer Thinning | High | Maintaining thickness uniformity, managing lithography overlay distortion, mechanical stress | High capital expenditure for advanced metrology and thinning equipment, steep yield learning curve | Advanced metrology for real-time thickness control, stress engineering, novel thinning processes |
| Wafer-to-Wafer Bonding Defectivity | High | Particle contamination, void formation, bond strength, precise alignment across large wafers | Significant investment in high-precision bonding tools, impact on early node yield and reliability | Ultra-cleanroom environments, advanced hybrid bonding equipment, in-situ defect inspection, process optimization |
| nTSV Defectivity | High | Open/short circuits, resistance variation, material compatibility, high aspect ratio etching challenges | Cost of defect localization and repair, impact on device reliability and overall yield | Optimized metallization processes (e.g., Ruthenium), advanced etch control, design-for-test (DFT) structures |
| Thermal Dissipation Challenges | Medium | Altered heat removal path due to backside power, potential for localized hot spots, increased junction temperature | Requires early co-design with advanced packaging, potential increase in cooling solution costs | Thermal co-design methodologies, advanced packaging solutions (e.g., direct backside contact), liquid cooling integration |
